## 1. Grammar progression by level (verified against the official wordlist)

The same structure recurs and deepens each level (a **spiral syllabus**):

| Area | Starters (Pre A1, ~4–7) | Movers (A1) adds | Flyers (A2) adds |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Verb tense** | present simple; present continuous (not for future) | past simple (regular + irregular); going-to future | present perfect; past continuous; will/might futures |
| **Modals** | `can` (ability/permission) | `could, must, shall, would` | `may, might, should, will` |
| **Possession/being** | `have got`; `there is/are`; `be` | — | — |
| **Nouns** | singular/plural incl. irregular; countable/uncountable | — | — |
| **Determiners** | `a/an/the/some`, `this/that/these/those` | `all, any, both, every, more, most` | `each, a few, a little, much, other` |
| **Prepositions of place** | `in front of, next to, under, behind, between` | `above, around, near, opposite, into, by` | `across, over, past, through` |
| **Comparison** | — | comparative/superlative adjectives | + adverbs; `as…as` |
| **Question words** | what, where, who, how many, how old | whose, which, how much/often | how long, why deepened |
| **Chunks** (taught whole, not parsed) | `Let's…`, `Would like…`, `Here you are`, `Me too` | — | — |

**App takeaway:** Starters grammar that fits touch mechanics = **plurals** (cat→cats),
**this/that**, **prepositions of place** (in/on/under), **`can` for ability**, **present
continuous** ("She is running"). These are the Grammar-island candidates.

## 2. Methodology for ages 4–7 (young-learner ELT principles)

- **Grammar is taught implicitly, through vocabulary and fixed chunks — not rules.** A 4–7 yo
  learns "I've got a cat" / "Let's go" as wholes; explicit metalanguage comes much later. This
  matches the app's no-reading, no-rules stance.
- **TPR (Total Physical Response):** link language to movement (touch your nose; jump; "put the
  ball *under* the box"). Movement + meaning aids retention (dual coding).
- **Spiral/recycling syllabus:** reintroduce each structure repeatedly at rising difficulty —
  exactly the app's per-skill levels + 30% review interleaving.
- **Listen-first, low-affective-filter, no fail state:** comprehension before production; lots of
  repetition with small variation; mistakes are scaffolded, never punished. Already core to the app.
- **Songs, chants, stories** carry rhythm and pattern (great for chunks/verb forms).
- **Drilling with variation** (substitution): "It's *in* the box → *on* the box → *under* the box."

## 3. Activities → app-mechanic mapping

| Grammar point | Classroom activity | App mechanic |
|---|---|---|
| **Plurals** (-s) | sort one vs. many | **tap-the-picture**: hear "cats" → tap the picture of *many* cats (1 vs 3) |
| **Prepositions of place** | put the toy in/on/under | **tap-the-picture**: hear "the cat is *under* the table" → tap the matching scene (4 scenes) |
| **this / that, these / those** | point near vs. far | **tap-the-picture**: near vs. far object |
| **`can` for ability** | mime + "I can swim" | **listen + tap**: "She can fly" → tap the bird, not the fish |
| **present continuous** | act it out, "I'm jumping" | **tap-the-scene** (4 actions) / **Speak**: answer "What is she doing?" |
| **sentence word order** | scrambled sentence strips | **build-from-tiles** (already the Flyer Write hut) — extend with grammar targets |
| **comparatives** (Movers) | line up by size | **tap**: "the *bigger* one" of two pictures |

Why these stick: **active recall** (produce, don't just recognize), **spaced repetition**
(interleaved review), **dual coding** (picture + audio + the word), **comprehensible input**
(audio just above current level with picture support).

## 4. Implications for the Grammar island

- Treat grammar like a theme: reuse the **same four huts** (Listen/Read/Write/Speak) — no new
  engine. A "grammar item" is just a question whose choices differ by a grammatical feature
  (singular vs plural picture; in vs on vs under scene), not by vocabulary.
- Start with **3 Starter-level structures**: plurals, prepositions of place, present continuous —
  all expressible as tap-the-correct-picture (the proven, no-reading mechanic).
- Scale the *same* structures up by level (more choices, sentence prompts at Flyer) via the
  existing level system, rather than inventing new mechanics.
